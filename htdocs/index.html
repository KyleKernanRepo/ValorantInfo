Iron

The very first rank section in Valorant is Iron. Iron consists of three ranks: Iron I, Iron II, and Iron III. At Iron, many players may lack the basic fundamentals of a competitive shooter. This can include map awareness, spray control, and tap firing. Players may also not understand how each agent works and how to counter them with their own utility. Executing sites can end up being very sloppy as well. Players in Iron may not focus on their economy, and when a save round is needed. Unfortunately, lower rank sections like Iron tend to contain lots of smurfs, which are experienced players who create new accounts to deceive others. For all ranks, you gain more or less ranked rating depending on the score of the match and your performance.

The third rank section in Valorant is Silver. Silver consists of three ranks: Silver I, Silver II, and Silver III. At Silver, players now have a good understanding of the game. This includes spray control and team play. You may see smarter economy plays like saving your gun for next round, or rushing into a site and risking a death to get a stronger financial benefit. Immortal

The second to last rank in Valorant is Immortal. Immortal consists of one rank and is ranked in the game leaderboard. At Immortal, you will expect semipro players and most of your top streamers, as well as upcoming players that are trying to make a name for themselves. In immortal you need to know how to play your agent to a tee. In this rank, you are only allowed to solo or duo queue to make the players focus on team play. The difference between Radiant and immortal is players that lack the team skill and or individual performance to go the extra mile. In Immortal there is little room for mistakes and if you aren't performing at your best you can easily find yourself deranked.

Radiant

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

The last rank section in Valorant is Radiant. Radiant does not have subranks within the rank itself. This rank is known for your top tier players like pros and streamers. To achieve this rank you need to climb the rank ladder, which is a hard task to accomplish for an everyday player. In radiant, you are only allowed to solo or duo queue. This is mainly because of the smaller player base at the higher rank. This forces players to have good team coordination and strategies on the fly. This is one of the reasons it's hard to stay radiant in Valorant. Most of the player base at the lower ranks likes to play as a five stack team for the best communication. Players at this rank also stay the same amount of elo(method for calculating the relative skill) regardless of how they play. This is good for players at radiant because of the common people that would focus on kills instead of the team aspect of winning the game.
